My Church in Bryan, TX

I volunteer at my church in a few different ways

I generally play guitar and organize live music sets in my church's small groups on Wednesday night and have only recently started serving in this way on sunday mornings. Getting to lead 20-30 people towards the love of Christ each week has been both humbling and fulfilling, and definitely my favorite experience since I've entered college.

Mixing the live sound on sunday mornings is a more recent way I have been able to help out. I've been able to grow a lot in my skills as an audio technician and interpersonally by challenging myself to know the people on stage. This interaction helps me give critical feedback to their performance after a live worship set.

My church uses 4 different cameras on sunday mornings to help cover different angles of the sanctuary for live broadcasts due to the pandemic. Every other week, I volunteer to help setup and monitor the cameras during the service, so that the viewing experience on the livestream is smooth and focused.

How I am using my education to serve

One of the ways I want to help my church in the near future is creating a software that automatically mixes their soundboard based on a prewritten database of songs that people can upload settings to. This will have a tremendous impact on small churches, schools, and any institution that has live music and doesn't have the money for a sound engineer or doesn't have the manpower for it.

This will be a revolutionary software, and I plan to make it free online to support churches and institutions who are broadcasting right now during the pandemic. Even after the pandemic, most churches will still be streaming so it will remain helpful. This is one way I plan to use my Computer Science education to build up the church of Christ.
